Instance Upgrade Experience
Role: Interaction designer
Team: 3 people including myself (a user researcher, interaction designer)
Techniques: wireframing, prototyping, usability testing

PROBLEM
Upgrades is a tedious process, however it is important for them to perform so that their hardware and software can have the updated features. They need a simple and intuitive way to upgrade their instance so that they can be up and running quickly.


ASSUMPTIONS
  • The admin needs a 24 hour period to test the upgrade.
  • They need to be informed about what is happening during the upgrade progress.

MOCKUP
I created wireframes and I met with the PM and architect to validate the technical aspect as well as to discuss the wireframes. Then I translated the wireframes into a hi-fi mockup.








USABILITY TESTING
My teammate and I along with our user researcher tested the designs by recruiting participants from our customer database. We specifically screened to find participants with background knowledge and experience managing appliances and deploying and configuring appliances. During the user research sessions, I actively took notes and asked questions to the participants. We tested 18 people through a Qualtrics survey combined with Adobe XD prototypes.



Highlights from the sessions were:
  • The upgrade flow was perceived as being straightforward and UI text message is helpful and easy to understand.
  • Different use cases have different requirements on the post-upgrade time window.
  • Actions on the instance details page are useful.
After we gathered all of the data, we had a debrief.

Based on the feedback that we received from the usability testing I iterated the designs and collaborated with my team to review my iterations during our readout with the product manager and engineering team.
